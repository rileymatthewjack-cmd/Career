The Thesis

"I will become the clinician-researcher who defines how AI-enabled electrophysiology devices and tools are rigorously validated and safely moved from prototype into real-world cardiac rhythm care — building the evidence infrastructure that lets promising AI-EP technology cross the translation gap safely, and using that authority to shape both the devices themselves and the standards by which they earn clinical trust."

Kill criteria — when this thesis gets revised, not just repeated
1. AI-EP validation commoditises into automated regulatory pathways that no longer need a clinician-authority.
2. Post-PhD, this research direction proves indistinguishable from several other UK clinician-researchers'.
3. Procedural EP excellence and this research direction turn out to be in genuine, unresolvable tension.
Tested explicitly at every Five-Year Reset (Systems tab) — not assumed away.

The Flywheel

How the four pillars actually connect

v1 sequenced four pillars as parallel tracks. A strategy compounds only if they're a loop — clinical work generating ideas that become funded research, that becomes technology, that changes patient care and reputation, that returns richer to clinical work.

EP clinical work→ Ideas→ Research→ Funding→ Industry→ Technology→ Patient care→ Reputation→ Opportunity→ EP clinical work

Every major decision should be checkable against one question: which node does this strengthen, and does it hand off cleanly to the next one? A project that dead-ends breaks the loop even if it looks productive alone.

Guiding principles

1. Every project is scored first against the thesis, then against the five domains (clinical / research / AI / innovation / teaching) — serving domains without serving the thesis is a hobby, fine, but label it honestly.
2. Never run an AI/technical project without a named supervisor, a defined output, and a line back to the thesis.
3. Choose posts and fellowships for the people, not just the institution — log the reasoning in the Decision Journal.
4. Revisit this panel on the full cadence (Systems tab) — weekly, monthly, quarterly, six-monthly, annually, and at the Five-Year Reset.
5. Keep two doors open over one marginally-better closed door, until ST4/5.

Systems, not years

The strategic review's core structural finding: a list of good intentions doesn't compound. These are the systems that make the plan self-correcting over 15 years instead of fixed at whatever felt right when it was written.

Personal Board of Directors

Governance, not just goodwill

A mentor list is goodwill. A board meets on a defined cadence and is shown the same review material used at the 6-month review, explicitly asked to challenge, not just advise.

Cadence checklist

This week / this month

The full Personal Operating System rhythm — weekly, monthly, quarterly, six-monthly, annual, Five-Year Reset. Tick off this cycle's items; they reset automatically next visit is up to you.

Relationship capital system

Tiered log, not a venue list

Tier 1 = active collaborator/supervisor-track, reviewed monthly. Tier 2 = warm contact, quarterly. Tier 3 = met once, annually. A contact turns red when its tier's review is overdue.

Research pipeline

Throughput, not a project list

A flat project list shows what exists. This shows whether the research engine is actually working — front-loaded (nothing finishing) or running dry (nothing new entering) are both visible here.

Decision journal

Calibrates every other system

Every 350+ opportunity, every explicit decline, the Route A/B call — logged with reasoning now, reviewed against the outcome later. This is the only way the scoring weights become genuinely calibrated rather than fixed at first guess.
